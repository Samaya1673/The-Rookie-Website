The Premise

It's never too late for a fresh start.

*The Rookie* stars Nathan Fillion as John Nolan, a man in his forties who, after a life-altering incident, decides to move to Los Angeles to become a police officer with the LAPD. As the oldest rookie on the force, he's met with skepticism from both his superiors and fellow officers.

The series chronicles his journey through the ranks, balancing the demanding and dangerous aspects of the job with the challenges of his personal life. Nolan must use his life experience, determination, and sense of humor to keep up with rookies twenty years his junior.

It's a compelling mix of police procedural, character-driven drama, and heartfelt moments, exploring what it means to find your calling later in life.

Did You Know?

Behind-the-scenes trivia for true fans.

The show is inspired by the true story of William "Bill" Norcross, who joined the LAPD in his mid-40s and is an executive producer on the show.

Nathan Fillion (John Nolan) is known for doing many of his own stunts, adding a layer of realism to his character's action sequences.

The show has a spin-off called *The Rookie: Feds*, starring Niecy Nash-Betts as Simone Clark, the oldest rookie in the FBI Academy.

Real police officers serve as consultants on set to ensure the authenticity of police tactics, radio chatter, and procedures depicted in the series.
